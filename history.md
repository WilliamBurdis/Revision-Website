# NORMANS
Q: What was the 'Harrying of the North'?
A: Campaigns by William (1069–70) to stop rebellions by destroying crops and livestock.
Q: What was the purpose of the Domesday Book?
A: To assess England's wealth for taxation and control.

# VIKINGS
Q: What was the 'Danelaw'?
A: Area of England where Viking laws/customs were dominant.
Q: Who was Alfred the Great?
A: King of Wessex who successfully defended against the Vikings.

# NAZIS
Q: What was the 'Enabling Act' (1933)?
A: Law giving Hitler power to make laws without the Reichstag.
Q: What was 'Gleichschaltung'?
A: The process of Nazi totalitarian control over all society.

# CRIME AND PUNISHMENT
Q: What was the 'Bloody Code'?
A: 18th-century period where 200+ crimes carried the death penalty.
Q: Who were the Bow Street Runners?
A: London's first professional police force (1749).
