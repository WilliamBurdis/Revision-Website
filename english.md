# --- MACBETH ---
Q: Who kills Macbeth?
A: Macduff.
Q: What is Macbeth's fatal flaw (hamartia)?
A: Vaulting Ambition.
Q: What do the witches prophesy about Banquo?
A: His sons will be kings, but he will not.
Q: "Fair is foul, and foul is ___"
A: Fair.
Q: Who says "Unsex me here"?
A: Lady Macbeth.
Q: What title does Macbeth receive after the Thane of Cawdor is executed?
A: Thane of Cawdor.
Q: Why can Macduff kill Macbeth despite the prophecy?
A: He was "from his mother's womb untimely ripp'd" (C-section).
Q: What does Lady Macbeth try to wash off her hands while sleepwalking?
A: Imaginary blood spots (guilt).
Q: Who is the King of Scotland at the start of the play?
A: King Duncan.
Q: Where is the play set?
A: Scotland.
Q: Who flees to England after Duncan's murder?
A: Malcolm.
Q: What does Macbeth see floating in the air before killing Duncan?
A: A dagger.
Q: Who says "Look like the innocent flower, but be the serpent under’t"?
A: Lady Macbeth.
Q: What is the main theme of the "Tomorrow and tomorrow and tomorrow" speech?
A: The futility and meaninglessness of life.
Q: Who is the goddess of witchcraft?
A: Hecate.
Q: What wood appears to move toward Dunsinane?
A: Birnam Wood.
Q: What are the three apparitions shown by the witches?
A: An armed head, a bloody child, and a crowned child holding a tree.
Q: Who kills Banquo?
A: Murderers hired by Macbeth.
Q: Whose ghost does Macbeth see at the banquet?
A: Banquo’s ghost.
Q: What does "Regicide" mean?
A: The act of killing a king.
Q: How does Lady Macbeth die?
A: Suicide (off-stage).
Q: Who is named the Prince of Cumberland (Duncan's heir)?
A: Malcolm.
Q: What does Macbeth mean when he says his mind is "full of scorpions"?
A: He is filled with anxiety and guilt.
Q: Why does Macbeth kill the servants/grooms?
A: To frame them for Duncan’s death and silence them.
Q: Who becomes King at the very end of the play?
A: Malcolm.

# --- A CHRISTMAS CAROL ---
Q: Who is the first ghost to visit Scrooge?
A: Jacob Marley.
Q: What does Marley’s chain represent?
A: The sins and greed he accumulated in life.
Q: What is Scrooge’s catchphrase?
A: "Bah! Humbug!"
Q: Who is Scrooge’s clerk?
A: Bob Cratchit.
Q: What is the name of Bob Cratchit’s youngest son?
A: Tiny Tim.
Q: Who is Scrooge's cheerful nephew?
A: Fred.
Q: Where does the Ghost of Christmas Past take Scrooge first?
A: To his old boarding school.
Q: Who was Scrooge’s kind employer when he was an apprentice?
A: Mr. Fezziwig.
Q: Why did Belle break her engagement to Scrooge?
A: Because he began to love money more than her.
Q: What does the Ghost of Christmas Present sit upon?
A: A throne of food (turkey, geese, fruit, etc.).
Q: Who are the two starved children hidden under the Present Ghost's robes?
A: Ignorance and Want.
Q: Which ghost does not speak?
A: The Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come.
Q: What does Scrooge see on the gravestone at the end of the visions?
A: His own name.
Q: What does Scrooge send to the Cratchit house on Christmas morning?
A: A giant prize turkey.
Q: How much did Scrooge give the charity collectors at the end of the book?
A: A very large sum, including back-payments.
Q: "I will honour Christmas in my ___, and try to keep it all the year."
A: Heart.
Q: What is the setting (city and era) of the novella?
A: Victorian London.
Q: Who says "God bless us, every one!"?
A: Tiny Tim.
Q: What was Scrooge’s business partner's name?
A: Jacob Marley.
Q: How many years ago did Marley die before the story starts?
A: Seven years.
Q: Scrooge is described as "solitary as an ___."
A: Oyster.
Q: What does the Ghost of Christmas Past look like?
A: Like a child, yet like an old man; with light coming from its head.
Q: What does the Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come resemble?
A: The Grim Reaper (clothed in black).
Q: How does the Cratchit family feel about Scrooge at the start?
A: They dislike him but toast him as the "Founder of the Feast."
Q: What does Scrooge do for Bob Cratchit the day after Christmas?
A: He gives him a raise.

# --- AN INSPECTOR CALLS ---
Q: What is the name of the girl who committed suicide?
A: Eva Smith (or Daisy Renton).
Q: In what year is the play set?
A: 1912.
Q: In what year was the play first performed?
A: 1945.
Q: What is Mr. Birling’s first name?
A: Arthur.
Q: Why did Mr. Birling sack Eva Smith?
A: She led a strike for higher wages.
Q: Where did Sheila Birling first encounter Eva Smith?
A: At Milwards (the dress shop).
Q: Why did Sheila get Eva fired?
A: Out of jealousy/spite after Eva looked better in a dress.
Q: Who is Gerald Croft?
A: Sheila’s fiancé and the son of a rival businessman.
Q: What was Eva Smith’s name when she met Gerald?
A: Daisy Renton.
Q: Where did Gerald meet Daisy Renton?
A: The Palace Variety Theatre (Stalls Bar).
Q: How did Eric Birling meet Eva?
A: At the same bar; he forced himself into her lodgings.
Q: What did Eric do that made the situation worse?
A: He stole money from his father's office to give to Eva.
Q: Who is the most "socialist" character by the end?
A: Sheila or Eric.
Q: What does the Inspector say we are all members of?
A: "One body."
Q: What is the Inspector's name?
A: Inspector Goole.
Q: Who is the "matriarch" of the family?
A: Mrs. (Sybil) Birling.
Q: Why did Mrs. Birling refuse to help Eva?
A: She found her "impertinent" for using the Birling name.
Q: Who is the father of Eva’s unborn child?
A: Eric Birling.
Q: What is the "Titanic" a metaphor for in Mr. Birling’s speech?
A: The arrogance of the upper class (the "unsinkable" society).
Q: What is the dramatic irony in Mr. Birling’s speech about war?
A: He says war is "impossible," but WWI started 2 years later.
Q: How does the play end?
A: The phone rings; a girl has died and a real inspector is coming.
Q: What is the Inspector’s main message?
A: Social responsibility (looking after one another).
Q: Does the Inspector turn out to be a real policeman?
A: No (at least not according to the local station).
Q: What is the name of the town the play is set in?
_A: Brumley.
Q: "Fire and blood and ___"
A: Anguish.
