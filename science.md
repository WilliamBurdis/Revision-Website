# --- BIOLOGY (Edexcel Spec) ---
Q: What is the magnification formula?
A: Magnification = Image size / Actual size.
Q: Describe the test for Starch.
A: Add Iodine solution; if positive, it turns from orange/brown to blue-black.
Q: What is an enzyme?
A: A biological catalyst made of protein that speeds up reactions.
Q: Where is amylase produced and what does it break down?
A: Produced in salivary glands/pancreas; breaks down starch into sugars.
Q: Define Diffusion.
A: Net movement of particles from an area of high to low concentration.
Q: Define Active Transport.
A: Movement of particles against a concentration gradient using energy (ATP).
Q: What are the stages of Mitosis?
A: Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase (PMAT).
Q: What is a stem cell?
A: An undifferentiated cell capable of becoming specialized cells.
Q: What is the function of the Myelin Sheath in a neurone?
A: Electrical insulation to speed up nerve impulse transmission.
Q: How do you calculate BMI?
A: BMI = mass (kg) / height² (m).

# --- CHEMISTRY (Edexcel Spec) ---
Q: What is the relative mass and charge of an electron?
A: Mass = 1/1835 (negligible); Charge = -1.
Q: How are elements arranged in the modern Periodic Table?
A: By increasing atomic number.
Q: What is an Alloy?
A: A mixture of two or more elements, at least one of which is a metal.
Q: Why do ionic compounds have high melting points?
A: Strong electrostatic forces of attraction between oppositely charged ions.
Q: Describe the structure of Diamond.
A: Giant covalent lattice; each carbon atom is bonded to 4 others.
Q: What is an Electrolyte?
A: An ionic liquid or solution that conducts electricity.
Q: What is the test for Oxygen gas?
A: A glowing splint relights.
Q: What is the general formula for Alkanes?
A: CnH2n+2.
Q: What is the test for Chlorine gas?
A: Damp blue litmus paper turns red then bleaches white.
Q: Define "Activation Energy".
A: The minimum energy needed for a reaction to occur.

# --- PHYSICS (Edexcel Spec) ---
Q: What is the difference between Weight and Mass?
A: Mass is the amount of matter (kg); Weight is the force of gravity (N).
Q: What is Newton's Second Law?
A: Force = Mass x Acceleration (F=ma).
Q: What is "Stopping Distance"?
A: Thinking Distance + Braking Distance.
Q: Define Conservation of Energy.
A: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred.
Q: What is the formula for Efficiency?
A: (Useful energy out / Total energy in) x 100.
Q: Which wave travels perpendicular to the direction of energy transfer?
A: Transverse waves.
Q: What is the order of the EM Spectrum (low to high frequency)?
A: Radio, Micro, Infrared, Visible, UV, X-ray, Gamma.
Q: What is the unit of Radioactivity?
A: Becquerel (Bq).
Q: What is Alpha radiation made of?
A: 2 protons and 2 neutrons (a Helium nucleus).
Q: What is the formula for Power (using Current and Voltage)?
A: Power = Current x Potential Difference (P=IV).
