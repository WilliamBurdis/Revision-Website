# BIOLOGY: CELLS AND CONTROL
Q: What is the function of mitochondria in an animal cell?
A: They are the site of aerobic respiration, releasing energy for the cell.

Q: Name the sub-cellular structures found in plant cells but NOT in animal cells.
A: Chloroplasts, vacuole, and a cellulose cell wall.

Q: Define 'prokaryotic' and give an example.
A: Cells without a nucleus or membrane-bound organelles. Example: Bacteria.

Q: What is the formula for calculating magnification?
A: Magnification = Image size / Actual size.

Q: Explain the role of haploid cells in reproduction.
A: Gametes (sperm/egg) are haploid (23 chromosomes) so that when they fuse, they form a diploid zygote (46).

Q: Define 'differentiation' in cells.
A: The process by which a cell changes to become specialised for its specific function.

# BIOLOGY: GENETICS & BIOENERGETICS
Q: What is a mutation?
A: A change in the base sequence of DNA, which can potentially change the phenotype.

Q: State the word equation for photosynthesis.
A: Carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen (in the presence of light and chlorophyll).

Q: Is photosynthesis an endothermic or exothermic reaction?
A: Endothermic (it takes in energy from the surroundings/light).

Q: What is the word equation for aerobic respiration?
A: Glucose + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water.

Q: Define 'Homeostasis'.
A: The maintenance of a constant internal environment (e.g., temperature, water, glucose).

# BIOLOGY: SYSTEMS & HEALTH
Q: What is the 'master gland' in the endocrine system?
A: The pituitary gland; it releases hormones that trigger other glands to release theirs.

Q: Explain how insulin controls blood glucose levels.
A: When blood glucose is high, the pancreas releases insulin, which causes the liver and muscles to move glucose from the blood into cells to be stored as glycogen.

Q: What is the function of the Xylem in a plant?
A: It transports water and mineral ions from the roots to the stem and leaves.

Q: Describe the test for reducing sugars (e.g., glucose).
A: Add Benedict’s reagent and heat in a water bath; a positive result turns from blue to brick-red.

Q: How do antibiotics work?
A: They inhibit cell processes in bacteria (e.g., cell wall synthesis) but do not affect host cells or viruses.

# CHEMISTRY: STATES & ATOMIC STRUCTURE
Q: Describe the arrangement of particles in a solid.
A: Regular lattice arrangement, fixed positions, vibrating around a point.

Q: What are the relative charges of protons, neutrons, and electrons?
A: Proton (+1), Neutron (0), Electron (-1).

Q: What is an isotope?
A: Atoms of the same element with the same number of protons but a different number of neutrons.

Q: Why are noble gases (Group 0) unreactive?
A: They have a full outer shell of electrons, making them stable.

Q: Explain 'Fractional Distillation' of crude oil.
A: Oil is heated; vapours rise and condense at different boiling points in a fractionating column.

# CHEMISTRY: BONDING & REACTIONS
Q: Describe ionic bonding.
A: The electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions, formed by the transfer of electrons from a metal to a non-metal.

Q: Why can graphite conduct electricity but diamond cannot?
A: Graphite has delocalised electrons that can move through the structure; diamond has all its outer electrons fixed in covalent bonds.

Q: What is the formula for calculating 'Moles'?
A: Moles = mass (g) / relative formula mass (Mr).

Q: State the general equation for neutralisation.
A: Acid + Base → Salt + Water.

Q: State Le Chatelier’s Principle.
A: If a change is made to a system at equilibrium, the system will move to counteract that change.

Q: What is the 'Flame Test' color for Potassium?
A: Lilac.

# PHYSICS: MOTION & FORCES
Q: State Newton's Second Law as an equation.
A: Force (N) = mass (kg) x acceleration (m/s²).

Q: What is the difference between mass and weight?
A: Mass is the amount of matter in an object (kg); Weight is the force of gravity acting on that mass (N).

Q: Define 'Stopping Distance'.
A: Thinking Distance + Braking Distance.

Q: State the formula for GPE (Gravitational Potential Energy).
A: GPE = mass (kg) x g (9.8 N/kg) x height (m).

Q: State Hooke’s Law.
A: The extension of a spring is directly proportional to the force applied ($F = k \times x$).

# PHYSICS: WAVES & RADIOACTIVITY
Q: State the wave speed equation.
A: Wave speed (v) = frequency (f) x wavelength (λ).

Q: List the EM spectrum in order of increasing frequency.
A: Radio, Microwave, Infrared, Visible, Ultraviolet, X-ray, Gamma.

Q: What is 'Half-life'?
A: The time taken for half of the undecayed nuclei in a radioactive sample to decay.

Q: Describe an Alpha (α) particle.
A: Two protons and two neutrons (a Helium nucleus); highly ionising but low penetration.

Q: What is 'Red-shift'?
A: The increase in the wavelength of light from distant galaxies, proving the universe is expanding.

# PHYSICS: ELECTRICITY & MAGNETISM
Q: State the equation linking Voltage, Current, and Resistance.
A: V = I x R.

Q: What is the function of a Step-up Transformer?
A: It increases the voltage (and decreases current) to reduce energy loss as heat in transmission lines.

Q: Describe the 'Motor Effect'.
A: When a current-carrying wire is placed in a magnetic field, the wire experiences a force.
